The Election Process

What to bring, what to know, and how to cast your ballot in Alabama.

What to Bring

Accepted Photo IDs

Alabama law requires every voter to present a valid photo ID. Any one of these will work.

🪪

Alabama Driver's License

Valid or expired less than 60 days. ALEA digital licenses accepted.

🏛️

Alabama Nondriver ID

State-issued nondriver ID card. Digital versions from ALEA accepted.

📘

U.S. Passport

A valid United States passport works at all Alabama polling locations.

🗳️

Alabama Photo Voter ID

Free from the Secretary of State, county Board of Registrars, or mobile ID unit.

🎖️

Government Employee ID

Photo employee ID from any branch of the U.S. government or State of Alabama.

🎓

College / University ID

Student or employee ID from any AL college or university. Digital student IDs accepted.

🔒

AL Corrections Release ID

Valid release temporary ID or movement/booking sheet with photo.

🌐

Other State-Issued ID

Photo ID from any other U.S. state, current or expired less than 60 days.

Don't Have a Photo ID?

Get a free Alabama Photo Voter ID card at your county Board of Registrars, the Secretary of State's office, or a mobile ID unit — no cost, year-round.

Know the Rules

Alabama Election Rules

Key rules governing Alabama elections.

1Voter Eligibility+

U.S. citizen, Alabama resident, at least 18 on or before Election Day. Cannot have been declared mentally incompetent or convicted of a disqualifying felony involving moral turpitude (unless voting rights have been restored).

2Registration Deadline — 14 Days Before+

Register online at the Secretary of State's website (if you have an AL driver's license or nondriver ID), in person at your county Board of Registrars, or at various government offices. New state residents may register the same day they move in but must still meet the 14-day cutoff.

3Polling Hours — 7 AM to 7 PM+

All Alabama polling places are open from 7:00 AM to 7:00 PM on Election Day. If you are in line by 7 PM, you have the right to cast your ballot regardless of wait time.

4No Early Voting+

Alabama does not offer early voting or in-person absentee voting. Absentee ballots require a qualifying reason, a copy of your photo ID with the application, and the completed ballot must be notarized or signed by two adult witnesses.

5Primary Crossover Rule+

Alabama has open primaries — you don't register by party. But if a runoff occurs, you must vote in the same party's runoff as the primary you participated in.

6Accessibility at the Polls+

All polling places must be accessible. Equipment to accommodate voters with disabilities is available at every location. A poll worker will ask if you need assistance.

7Address Changes+

Same precinct: no update needed. New precinct, same county: provisional ballot at new location. New county: must re-register before the deadline.

Step by Step

How to Vote on Election Day

1. Confirm Registration & Polling Place

Use the Secretary of State's "My Voting Information" tool online.

2. Bring Your Photo ID

Must be valid or expired fewer than 60 days. Physical ID required (except ALEA digital licenses and digital student IDs).

3. Go to Your Polling Place (7 AM – 7 PM)

If you're in line by 7 PM, you will be allowed to vote.

4. Present Your ID

A poll worker checks your ID against the voter roll and provides your ballot.

5. Cast Your Ballot

Mark your choices and feed it into the tabulation machine.

No ID? You Can Still Vote

Provisional Ballot Options

👤

Two Officials Verify You

If two election officials positively identify you, they sign an affidavit and you vote normally.

📋

Provisional Ballot

Present valid photo ID to the Board of Registrars by 5 PM on Friday after the election.

🆓

Free Voter ID

Available year-round at your county Board of Registrars or a mobile ID unit.
